Why 180 total, and why 60 is the pickleball ceiling

The two memberships scale differently — on purpose.

Recovery (120 members at maturity) uses the pool, jacuzzi, cold plunge, saunas, steam, and gym — none of which consume court time. This tier is scalable. If demand warrants, the soft cap can flex above 120. Exclusivity is maintained through pricing and member curation, not artificial ceilings. By comparison, Equinox Brickell runs thousands of members on comparable wellness facilities. 120 is conservative scarcity.

Club (60 members at maturity) is the pickleball tier. This number is hard-capped by court availability. Every Club member gets 6 free off-peak booking hours per month — at 60 members that's 360 free hours/month, all concentrated in the off-peak 11am–4pm weekday window, which means peak revenue hours (mornings before 11am, evenings after 4pm, and weekends) stay fully available for non-member paid bookings and events. Going above 60 overwhelms off-peak capacity and starts eating into peak revenue.

How court bookings actually work

One person reserves, but everyone can pay. PodPlay's booking software lets the reservation holder invite up to 3 others — the system splits the charge across everyone automatically.

A Club member booking an off-peak free slot gets it free. If they bring 3 fellow members, everyone plays free. If they bring non-members, those non-members pay guest rate through the split-payment system.

Members should always be the one reserving — it unlocks the best pricing for the whole group.

Why weekend priority is the real perk

Saturday and Sunday are the scarcest real estate in the club. Club members book weekends 7 days in advance. Recovery members and non-members cannot book weekends until 24 hours before tee-off — and only if Club members haven't grabbed the slots.

This is how premium clubs protect peak revenue while making the Club tier the obvious upgrade. It's not about unlimited free play — it's about guaranteed access when it matters.

The "guest" perk, clarified

There are two different uses of "guest":

Guests on a court booking — non-members who join a member's reservation. They play, and unless it's the member's free off-peak slot, they pay their share through split-payment.

Guest passes (the actual perk) — Recovery members get 2 passes/month, Club members get 4 passes/month to bring a non-member to experience the full recovery stack — no court booking required. This is how referrals happen.

Why Club tier is the obvious upgrade

A Club member playing 3×/week at peak (12 sessions/mo):
• 4 free off-peak = $0
• 8 peak at $45/hr = $360

Same player as non-member: 12 × $80 = $960 just for courts, no recovery.

Club member saves $300/mo and gets the recovery stack. That's what makes the upgrade feel like a bargain.

03 The Build

Two paths. Same court.
Different timing.

An enclosed pickleball court in Miami has one real challenge: summer heat. A glass box without climate control becomes unplayable June through September — killing half the revenue year. We present two options: build the full climate-controlled version on Day One, or stage the roof + AC as a Year 2 upgrade once revenue is proven.

Option A · Hero Build

Roof + Climate Control
on Day One

16–18 WEEKS · PLAYS YEAR-ROUND
$340K
Range: $315K–$370K · includes smart court tech
  • Full enclosure · 25ft glass + mesh + polycarbonate
  • Fixed-roof structure · steel frame + insulated panels
  • Mini-split AC + mechanical ventilation
  • Acoustic baffling throughout
  • Musco/LSI LED sport lighting
  • Plays in any weather, any season
Trade-off: Higher upfront cost, longer build timeline. But year-round playability protects ~40% of summer revenue that the staged version loses, and the club can market as a true all-weather facility from launch — which drives faster membership sell-through and faster payback.

Trade-off: Lower upfront cost and faster to open. But expect ~15–20% seasonal revenue loss during summer peak heat and ~10% of bookings lost to rain. Total 3-year cost is roughly the same once the Year 2 upgrade is added — so this option mainly spreads CapEx, not reduces it.
Miami Reality Check

Why a roof without AC isn't on the table.

Miami summer highs average 90°F+ from June through September with 70–80% humidity. A glass enclosure traps heat — unventilated, interior temps hit 110°F+ by midday. That's heat-stroke territory, not a premium club experience. If we're building an enclosure, it needs AC, or it needs no roof.

Option A solves this with mini-split AC + ventilation, allowing year-round play. Option B stays open-top so summer heat escapes, accepting seasonal revenue loss and adding the AC-controlled roof in Year 2 once cashflow justifies it. There is no middle path — a closed roof without AC breaks the product.

04 The Tech

The only court in Miami
with a smart scoreboard
and instant replay.

PodPlay Pro smart court tech — the same system used by Paddle + Par (Beverly Hills, MI), Ace Pickleball Club, and premium clubs across the country. Digital scoreboard visible from anywhere on the court. Instant replay for any great point — saved to players' phones. Members leave with shareable highlights every session. No other court in Miami has this.

01

Digital Scoreboard

Wristband buttons track score, server, and side automatically. Score displayed on a large monitor visible from anywhere on court. No more "what's the score?" mid-rally.

02

Instant Replay

One tap on the Apple Watch or clip-on remote saves the last 30 seconds to the cloud. Players walk off the court with pro-quality highlight clips of their best points — emailed to them automatically.

03

Integrated Booking

PodPlay replaces the third-party booking software entirely. Members book, pay, split charges, and access the building — all from one white-labeled DUA Pickleball app. Branded end-to-end.

04

Social Content Machine

Every member who makes a great point shares it. Organic social media exposure for DUA — free marketing that drives both pickleball awareness and hotel brand lift. This is how content engines are built.

PodPlay's monitors are designed as digital ad real estate. Every instant replay plays with a "Presented by [Sponsor]" tag. Every idle moment between points displays sponsor logos. Every time a player taps save, a branded overlay appears.

Industry benchmark: $3,000–$15,000 per year per sponsor. A single court at DUA could carry 3–5 sponsors (paddle brand, beverage, athletic apparel, recovery supplement, financial services) generating $15K–$40K/year in sponsorship revenue on top of membership and court revenue. 08 The Valuation

The multiplier effect on
property value.

Brickell boutique hotels trade at 6–8% cap rates. Every dollar of recurring NOI is worth roughly $14 of property value at a 7% cap. A capped membership club with recurring dues and a waitlist isn't just revenue — it's a capitalizable, transferable asset.

Lower cap rate = higher property value. Signed annual membership contracts compress cap rates.

The membership roster is a transferable asset.

180 memberships with annual contracts, a waitlist, and recurring dues is exactly the kind of asset that compresses cap rates. Buyers underwrite it. Lenders underwrite it. It signals DUA isn't just a 124-key boutique — it's a community with cashflow.

Miami hasn't built this category yet. Being first in Brickell with a capped, curated pickleball-and-recovery club is the kind of moat that shows up in the exit multiple, not just the P&L.

Quick note on cap rates

Cap rate is the yield a buyer demands. Lower cap = buyer is willing to pay more for the same NOI. Higher cap = buyer demands more yield. Brickell boutique hotels trade at the lower end because they're high-demand assets.

NOI ÷ cap rate = property value. So $494K NOI ÷ 6% = $8.2M. ÷ 7% = $7.1M. ÷ 8% = $6.2M. Lower cap = higher value.

Why this stacks with the PNC partnership

PNC drives wellness-crowd awareness. The pickleball club converts that awareness into recurring membership revenue. They reinforce each other — PNC events become member acquisition, members become PNC event regulars. One hotel, two halo systems, compounding.

Why now, before someone else does it

DETA has 17 courts in West Miami. Dinko has indoor courts in Upper Eastside. Sip & Pickle is in Wynwood. Nobody has a capped, luxury, rooftop, recovery-integrated club in Brickell. That window is open right now. In 18 months, it won't be.

Why phased pricing matters

Founding rates create urgency and evangelists. Intro proves demand. Final establishes premium positioning. By the time we're at Final pricing, we have waitlists — which is when Alan realizes the real value isn't the court, it's the recurring revenue asset attached to his building.

Why the recovery stack is the moat

Any hotel can add a court. Only a handful can bundle a court with pool, outdoor jacuzzi, cold plunge, saunas, steam, gym, towel service — already built, already licensed, already staffed. The amenities DUA already owns make membership worth $299, not $80.
